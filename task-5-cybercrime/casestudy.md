## Cybercrime Case Study – Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers try to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, and banking details by pretending to be a trusted entity. It usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites that look genuine.

A common example is when a student receives an email that appears to be from a bank or a popular website. The email may contain a message like “Your account will be blocked. Click here to verify.” When the user clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website that looks identical to the real one. The user then enters their login details, which are captured by the attacker.

Students and young users are often targeted because they may not always verify the authenticity of such messages. The consequences can include loss of personal data, financial loss, and misuse of identity.

Phishing attacks are increasing rapidly, and awareness is the best way to prevent them. Users must always verify the source of emails and avoid clicking on suspicious links.
